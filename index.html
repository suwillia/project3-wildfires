Overview:

The January 2025 series of wildfires in Southern California has only been recently contained, and the wide-reaching impact of the destruction is still being determined.

With that in mind, our group wanted to use our Data Visualization project to examine data tracking wildfires in the United States from 2014-2020. We visualized data on wildfire size, count and location using Plotly and Leaflet, and created an API housing the data using Flask.

For this project, we used the National Interagency Fire Occurrence Sixth Edition 1992-2020 (Feature Layer), a dataset provided by the US Forest Service, an agency within the US Department of Agriculture and housed on data.gov.

Ethical Considerations:

Data Privacy

We have carefully avoided including any identifying information in our data set.All data used in our visualizations comes from the USDA's data source, ensuring accuracy and credibility

Sensitivity and Objectivity

We are acutely aware that wildfires are a sensitive subject, as they have a devastating effect on people, property and wildlife. We sought to visualize the data in an objective, non-sensationalized manner.

Importance of Controlled Burns

We recognise that prescribed, controlled fires are an effective way to reduce wildfire risk, destroy invasive vegetation, and improve habitats. However, our visualizations specifically focus on wildfires, not on controlled burns.
